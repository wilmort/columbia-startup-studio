Researcher's Stance

V1 is a massive lift from baseline across every metric ‚Äî conversion from 7% to 43%, resonance from 17% to 58%, clarity from 0% to 88% nailed_it. The brand voice is working. However, two critical gaps emerged: fomo_scroller has a 75% resonance / 17% conversion gap (they love the concept but the backward-looking copy doesn't serve their forward-looking need), and journal_graveyard is stuck at 17% (retention concerns are unsolved). Network believability at 62% is the breakout metric ‚Äî this is the product's differentiator.

Executive Summary

First real copy version tested. Identity-driven hero ('Your social life, ranked'), specific examples (8/10 Thai place), one-tap emphasis, network discovery section. All metrics lifted massively from baseline. Two structural gaps identified: fomo_scroller needs forward-looking reframe, journal_graveyard needs retention proof.

Findings

  • +36pp conversion lift validates the baseline approach: V1 at 43% vs baseline 7%. The floor was real, the lift is real.
  • Network discovery is the breakout concept: 62% believability ‚Äî highest metric. Friend-powered discovery resonates more than the personal journal framing.
  • Resonance-conversion gap in fomo_scroller: 75% resonance but only 17% conversion. They love the concept but the copy is backward-looking ('record what you did'). They need 'discover what to do next.'
  • Clarity at 88% nailed_it: The specific examples and Beli comparison communicate the product clearly. Major improvement from 0%.
  • Price perception improved: 'too expensive' dropped from 50% to 23%. Value framing makes $12/month feel justified.

Recommendations

  1. Reframe hero for forward-looking discovery: Add 'discover what's next' to subhead for fomo_scroller.
  2. Add retention section: Address journal_graveyard's 'I'll abandon this in a week' concern directly.
  3. Address cold-start: Multiple buckets ask 'what if I'm the first of my friends?' Add pre-network value paragraph.
  4. Soften paywall: Social features behind Pro creates a catch-22. Add basic friend activity to free.
  5. Add referral incentive: Align business model with network growth.

Qualitative Themes

  • Memory framing lands universally (28 mentions): 'Hundreds of places, remember twenty' is the most-cited problem statement.
  • 'One tap' is critical (22 mentions): Especially for journal_graveyard. The effortlessness claim is the entry point.
  • Network discovery excites (31 mentions): 'Friends' picks over algorithms' is the differentiator personas respond to most.
  • Cold-start concern (15 mentions): 'What if none of my friends use this?' across 4 of 5 buckets.
  • Social paywall objection (12 mentions): 'Don't gate the social features behind Pro ‚Äî the network can't grow.'

Clarity Notes

88% nailed_it, 10% partial, 2% wrong. Major improvement. The Beli comparison and specific examples communicate the product clearly.
