# Lean Canvases - Top 3 Concepts

## Canvas #1: ü•á Spaced Repetition for Algorithm Patterns

### Problem (Top 3)
1. **Pattern Blindness**: Students solve 200+ LeetCode problems but can't recognize which pattern applies during actual interviews
2. **No Review System**: No systematic way to recall "what was the trick?" for problems solved weeks/months ago
3. **Memorization ‚â† Understanding**: Interviews test pattern recognition speed, not solution memorization

### Solution (Top 3 Features)
1. **Smart Resurfacing**: Algorithm shows you problems at spaced intervals (1 week, 1 month, 3 months) based on forgetting curve
2. **Pattern-First Review**: Must identify the pattern category BEFORE seeing your old solution
3. **Offline Mobile-First**: Works on subway with no internet; sync when connected

### Unique Value Proposition
**"Stop memorizing 200 solutions. Start recognizing 15 patterns."**

Duolingo-style spaced repetition for coding interviews. Forces you to recall the pattern before showing the solution. Works offline on your commute.

### Unfair Advantage
- Columbia CS network = 500+ target users for rapid testing
- Founders ARE the users (dogfooding from day 1)
- Academic research on spaced repetition validates approach
- First-mover in "LeetCode + spaced repetition" intersection

### Customer Segments
**Primary:**
- Columbia MS CS students (3-6 month interview prep timeline)

**Secondary:**
- Bootcamp graduates preparing for tech roles
- Self-taught developers doing career transitions
- Anyone doing LeetCode who wants better retention

### Channels
1. **Direct access**: Columbia CS GroupMe, Slack, email lists
2. **Organic**: r/cscareerquestions, Blind forums, TikTok
3. **Partnerships**: Columbia career center, CS department
4. **SEO**: "LeetCode spaced repetition", "algorithm pattern review"

### Cost Structure
- Mobile app development (React Native): **$15-25K** one-time
- LeetCode API/data infrastructure: **$3K/year**
- Cloud hosting (Firebase): **$200/month** ‚Üí **$500/month** at scale
- Part-time student developers: **$5K/semester**
- **Total Year 1**: ~$35K

### Revenue Streams
1. **Freemium**: 20 problems free, **$10/month** unlimited ($120/year)
2. **University licenses**: **$500/semester** for career centers to offer students
3. **Premium features**: AI hints, mock interview mode (**$5/month** add-on)

**Break-even math**: Need ~300 paying users at $10/month = $3K/month

### Key Metrics
1. **WAU** (Weekly Active Users) - engagement signal
2. **Problems reviewed per session** - depth of use
3. **Pattern recall accuracy over time** - learning efficacy
4. **Free ‚Üí Paid conversion rate** - business viability
5. **NPS from students who got offers** - outcome validation

## Canvas #2: ü•à AI Mock Interviewer (Voice)

### Problem (Top 3)
1. **No Pressure Practice**: Students practice problems at their own pace, but interviews are 45-min time-boxed with high stress
2. **Scheduling Friction**: Mock interviews with peers are valuable but hard to coordinate consistently
3. **Commute Dead Time**: Can't practice "thinking out loud" or handling hints during 30-45 min subway rides

### Solution (Top 3 Features)
1. **Voice-Based Mock Interviews**: AI plays interviewer role via earbuds - asks problem, listens to your approach, gives hints
2. **Real-Time Feedback**: Identifies when you're stuck, offers graduated hints like a real interviewer would
3. **Post-Interview Report**: Reviews communication clarity, approach quality, edge cases missed, time management

### Unique Value Proposition
**"Practice interviews daily, not just once before the big day."**

AI interviewer you can practice with anytime, anywhere (subway, gym, walking). Works via voice with earbuds. Gives real-time hints and detailed feedback after.

### Unfair Advantage
- Voice AI tech matured in 2024-2025 (wasn't viable 2 years ago)
- Founders experienced interview anxiety firsthand - deep empathy
- Columbia beta testing pool for rapid iteration
- Network effects: "I practiced with AI and got my offer" social proof

### Customer Segments
**Primary:**
- Columbia MS CS students (Sept-March recruiting season)

**Secondary:**
- Bootcamp graduates lacking practice partners
- Self-taught developers doing career transitions
- Anyone wanting behavioral + technical interview practice

### Channels
1. **B2C Direct**: Columbia CS career services demos, student GroupMe
2. **Content**: YouTube/TikTok showing "subway practice" sessions
3. **Integration**: Partner with LeetCode, Pramp, interviewing.io
4. **Word-of-mouth**: Early users share success stories

### Cost Structure
- Voice recognition API (Whisper): **$0.10-0.30/hour**
- LLM costs (GPT-4): **~$0.50 per 45-min interview**
- Mobile app development: **$20-30K** one-time
- Cloud infrastructure: **$500/month** at scale
- **Variable cost per user**: ~$2-3/month (AI costs)

### Revenue Streams
1. **Subscription**: **$15/month** for unlimited interviews
2. **Pay-per-interview**: **$3/session** for casual users
3. **B2B**: Bootcamps/universities at **$50/student/semester**

**Unit economics**: $15/month revenue - $3 AI costs = $12 gross margin per user (80% margin)

### Key Metrics
1. **Interviews completed per user per week** - engagement
2. **User-reported helpfulness score** - product-market fit
3. **Free trial ‚Üí Paid conversion** - monetization
4. **Correlation: # mock interviews ‚Üí offer rate** - value proof

## Canvas #3: ü•â Credit Card Rewards Explainer

### Problem (Top 3)
1. **Opacity**: US credit card rewards systems (points, categories, rotating bonuses, statement credits) are deliberately confusing
2. **Money Left on Table**: International students make suboptimal card choices or don't activate quarterly categories - losing $200-500/year
3. **Information Overload**: Reddit/blogs are too dense; students want "just tell me what to do"

### Solution (Top 3 Features)
1. **Card Analyzer**: Link/upload your cards ‚Üí see ALL benefits in plain English on one dashboard
2. **Monthly Spending Optimizer**: "Use Chase for dining, Amex for groceries this month" with exact $ value shown
3. **Smart Notifications**: "Your Discover rotating category changed to gas stations - tap to activate now"

### Unique Value Proposition
**"If you use THIS card for groceries this month, you get $18 back‚Äîwe'll show you the math."**

Plain-English translator for credit card rewards. Analyzes your cards and spending, tells you exactly which card to use for what. Built for international students who didn't grow up with this system.

### Unfair Advantage
- Founders ARE international students - perfect user empathy
- Columbia has 8,000+ international students for testing
- Network effects: Users refer new arrivals each semester
- Content moat: No one explains this simply for non-US natives

### Customer Segments
**Primary:**
- International graduate students (F-1 visa, limited credit history)

**Secondary:**
- Recent immigrants navigating US financial system
- Young professionals opening first rewards cards
- Anyone confused by credit card marketing

### Channels
1. **Campus**: International student orientation at Columbia, NYU, MIT
2. **Partnerships**: International student offices white-label version
3. **Content**: YouTube explainer videos "Credit Cards 101 for International Students"
4. **Referral**: Users invite new arrivals, earn bonus month free

### Cost Structure
- Web app development: **$10-15K** initial
- Credit card data APIs (Plaid): **$500/month**
- Content creation (videos): **$2K** one-time
- Student marketing: **$1K/semester**
- **Total Year 1**: ~$25K

### Revenue Streams
1. **Freemium**: Basic card analysis free, **$5/month** for spending optimization
2. **Affiliate commissions**: **$50-200 per approved** credit card application
3. **University B2B**: **$1,000/year** for white-labeled version

**Monetization thesis**: Even 10% affiliate conversion on free users > subscription revenue

### Key Metrics
1. **MAU** (Monthly Active Users)
2. **Credit cards added per user** - engagement depth
3. **Estimated rewards captured** ($/user/year) - value metric
4. **Affiliate conversion rate** - main revenue driver
5. **User testimonials**: "I earned $X more this year"

## Strategic Analysis Across All Three Canvases

### Common Success Factors
1. **Network Effects**: All three benefit from word-of-mouth in tight-knit Columbia community
2. **Timing Advantage**: AI maturity (2024-2025) enables solutions that weren't viable 2 years ago
3. **Dogfooding**: We are the target users for all concepts - eliminates user research risk
4. **Low CAC**: Direct access to 500+ potential users through campus channels
5. **Software-Only**: Minimal variable costs (AI tokens, APIs) - highly scalable
6. **Fast Validation**: Can run beta tests within 2 weeks of building MVP

### Unit Economics Comparison

| Concept | Monthly Revenue/User | Variable Cost/User | Gross Margin |

| Spaced Repetition | $10 | ~$0.50 | 95% |

### Next Steps: Validation Plan

**Week 1-2: User Interviews (n=20 per concept)**
- Quantify pain: "Rate this problem 1-10"
- Current solutions: "What do you do today?"
- Willingness to pay: "Would you pay $X for this?"

**Week 3: Rapid Prototyping**
- Spaced Repetition: Figma prototype of mobile flow
- AI Mock Interviewer: Wizard of Oz test (human plays AI)
- Credit Card Explainer: Notion page with card comparisons

**Week 4: Concept Selection**
Choose ONE based on:
- 15+ out of 20 people rate pain as 8+/10
- 10+ people provide email for launch notification
- 3+ people commit to paying at launch
- Zero technical feasibility blockers

### Team Conviction
All three concepts solve real problems we've personally experienced. We have direct access to our target users and unique advantages to build these solutions. We're ready to move fast on validation and commit 12 weeks to the winning concept.
