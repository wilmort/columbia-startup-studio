# Interview #8 Analysis -- Irene

## 1. Participant Profile

-   **Name:** Irene\
-   **Role:** Software Engineer at Netflix\
-   **Location:** New York City\
-   **Social Frequency:** \~3 times per week\
-   **Weekend Pattern:** Friends, events, restaurants, low-commitment
    social plans

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2. Core Behavioral Patterns

### Discovery Channels

-   Friends (primary source)
-   Instagram stories (passive discovery)
-   Word-of-mouth recommendations
-   Directly consulting "experts" in her friend group

### Social Structure Insight

She has: - A "restaurant friend" - A "concert friend"

This implies: - Micro-experts exist within friend groups. - Trust is
role-based, not platform-based.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3. Core Pain Points

### 1. Organic Social Discovery

> "It's kind of hard to meet new people in the city in a more natural,
> organic way."

She wants: - Low-commitment, small group events - Hobby exploration
without heavy social pressure - Casual discovery environments

This is less about *choosing events* and more about *expanding network
surface area*.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 2. Event Awareness Timing Problem

> "Sometimes you don't even realize there's some fun event going on
> until it passed."

This is significant.

She is not primarily frustrated by: - Too many options - Poor vibe
filtering

She is frustrated by: - Missing time-sensitive events - Ephemeral event
visibility

This suggests: - Notification timing matters - Real-time surfacing \>
static reviews

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4. Logging & Incentives Insight

Unlike Tiffany (Interview #7), Irene:

-   Would rate things **if incentivized**
-   Responds to:
    -   Points systems
    -   Exclusive access
    -   Tangible rewards
-   Prefers extremely low-friction input (star rating, like/dislike)

She does NOT want: - Long-form reviews - Frequent logging of everything

She would contribute if: - Easy - Rewarded - Improves personalization

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 5. Desired App Features

If an app existed, she would want:

1.  Simple star/like input
2.  Clear feedback loop ("more input = better recommendations")
3.  Trending events section
4.  Personalized recommendations
5.  Friend feed layer
6.  Ability to follow taste leaders

Preferred discovery method: \> Combination of friend feed +
personalization

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 6. Emotional Signals

-   Interest level: Moderate-to-High
-   Reaction to predictive app: "I think I would use it."

This is stronger than passive curiosity. Still not urgent, but shows
behavioral openness.

## 8. Strategic Implications

### Strong Signals

-   Incentives can drive contribution
-   Friend micro-experts are real behavioral anchors
-   Real-time event surfacing may be more valuable than retrospective
    reviews
-   Low-friction UX is mandatory

### Potential Direction

Rather than "social Yelp," this may need to be:

-   A **real-time social signal layer**
-   With:
    -   Friend visibility
    -   Trending local events
    -   Time-sensitive nudges
    -   Simple feedback loops
    -   Optional gamification
