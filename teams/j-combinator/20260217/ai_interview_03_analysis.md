## 3. Core Pain Points Identified

### 1️⃣ Fragmentation + Labor

She explicitly says she would love consolidation similar to OpenTable.

Key comparison insight:

OpenTable works because:
- Centralized info
- Reviews
- Reservation built in
- Low effort

She wants similar consolidation for broader social experiences.

---

### 2️⃣ Too Many Options → Hard to Narrow

She says the biggest pain point is:

> "Finding what to do... because in a city that has a lot of options, there are a lot of options."

This mirrors Interview #1’s overload signal.

The issue is not scarcity.  
It is filtering.

---

### 3️⃣ Geographic Constraint

She stays mostly in:
- Brooklyn
- Lower Manhattan

She wishes she explored more neighborhoods.

Barrier = travel friction + habit loop.

Opportunity:
Neighborhood-expansion nudges.

---

## 4. Review & Rating Behavior

Important signal:

She:
- Saves things
- Shares with friends
- Does NOT leave reviews

This is now 2 interviews (2 & 3) confirming:

Users do not want to write reviews.

Rating-heavy systems may not scale.

Preference learning should rely on:
- Saves
- Clicks
- Attendance
- Social signals

Not written reviews.

---

## 5. Algorithm Reaction

She is open to predictive suggestions if:
- They align with her saved history
- They feel personalized

Trust depends on relevance.

Weak recommendations would quickly erode trust.

## 6. Social Experience Preference

Very important nuance:

She wants activities that:
- Blend social groups
- Allow meeting new people
- Do not require a large group
- Allow bringing 1 "comfort person"

This is distinct from nightlife.

This is:
- Structured social experiences
- Light commitment
- Semi-curated gatherings

Example she gives:
Bring a friend + painting activity + meet others.

This suggests interest in:
- Social hobby mixers
- Creative workshops
- Community-building events

## 8. Strategic Implications

### Product Should:

- Consolidate fragmented sources
- Reduce browsing labor
- Provide neighborhood-based suggestions
- Surface semi-structured social activities
- Not require review writing

### Strong Feature Signals:

- Save-based preference learning
- Neighborhood filters
- "Bring one friend"-friendly activities
- Consolidated discovery feed
- Option narrowing (not option expansion)
