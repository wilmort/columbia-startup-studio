# Strength Training App – Athlete Interviews

## Interview 04 – Noa Klein

### Raw Interview Notes

**Background & Lifting Habits**
- Athlete on the Columbia Women’s Rowing team  
- Genuinely enjoys lifting; sees it as more than just a required team activity  
- Lifted independently over the summer without direct supervision  
- Comfortable doing both structured team lifts and fully self-guided sessions  
- Takes personal responsibility for her own strength development  

**Current Tracking Behavior**
- Uses TrainHeroic as her primary tracking app  
- Originally introduced to TrainHeroic by her high school coach  
- Continues using her high school account because her high school coach still pays for the team subscription  
- Logs Columbia team lifts manually into TrainHeroic on her own  
- Chooses to track even when not required by current coaches  
- Values having a record of past lifts  

**Motivations for Tracking**
- Wants to clearly see if she is improving over time  
- Uses tracking as a way to hold herself accountable  
- Looks back at previous workouts to confirm progress  
- Finds reassurance in data  

**Features She Likes in TrainHeroic**
- Interface feels intuitive and easy to navigate  
- Able to log lifts in real time during workouts  
- Can easily review past workouts  
- Can see long-term trends, weight increases, and PRs  

**Unmet Needs / Desired Features**
- Frequently records videos of her lifts to review form  
- Currently stores videos separately in her camera roll  
- Wants ability to attach videos directly to specific lifts  
- Would like videos tied to exact dates and weight amounts  
- Wants a built-in rest timer within the workout screen  
- Prefers not to switch between apps for timing  
- Likes the social aspect of Strava  
- Wants lifting to feel more social or visible  
- Interested in seeing what teammates are doing  
- Feels messaging functionality in current tools could be improved  

**Concerns About Adoption**
- Thinks it would be difficult to get an entire team to switch apps  
- Notes that athletes are resistant to change  
- Believes many teammates will only switch if the new app is clearly superior  
- Recognizes onboarding friction as a real barrier

## Interview 05 – Olivia McManus

### Raw Interview Notes

**General Attitude Toward Tracking**
- Does not currently track her weights  
- Has never built a consistent tracking habit  
- Open to tracking only if extremely simple  
- Expressed: “I want to press as few buttons as possible.”  

**Friction Sensitivity**
- Does not want to manually input multiple fields per set  
- Would likely stop using an app if it required too many taps  
- Prefers streamlined, minimal interaction  

**Relationship to Coach & Structure**
- Wants coach to be in control of programming  
- Prefers coach to input PRs  
- Prefers coach to define improvement goals  
- Prefers coach to upload the full detailed workout plan  
- Wants to be guided step-by-step through workouts  
- Does not want to make decisions about progression  

**Desired Experience**
- Shows up and executes the workout  
- Minimal thinking required during session  
- Clear instructions from coach  
- Tracking would be “nice,” but only if effortless

## Interview Snapshot – Olivia McManus

## Interview 06 – Isa Bessette

### Raw Interview Notes

**Feelings About Progress**
- Feels unsure about how she is progressing in the gym  
- Does not know what rate of progress is expected  
- Wonders if she is improving fast enough  
- Lacks clarity on benchmarks  

**Pain Points**
- Feels “lost” when thinking about strength development  
- Does not have organized records of past lifts  
- Cannot easily compare current performance to past performance  
- Lacks feedback loop  

**Desired Features**
- Wants to see expected rate of progression  
- Wants to compare her performance to team standards or benchmarks  
- Wants to know whether she is on track  
- Wants more organization in one place  
- Wants coach-driven accountability  
- Wants to be pushed by coach  

**Ease of Use Preference**
- Wants to press as few buttons as possible  
- Does not want complicated logging  
- Prioritizes simplicity  

**Competitive Component**
- Interested in friendly competition with teammates  
- Would like visibility into others’ progress  
- Motivated by comparison  
- Sees competition as a way to push herself

## Interview Snapshot – Isa Bessette
