# Customer Interview Guide  
**Problem Space:** Wardrobe management, outfit decision fatigue, fit confidence, and shopping friction  
**Target Audience:** Graduate students and young professionals

---

## 1. Opening (2 minutes)

Hi, I’m working on a class project at Columbia where we’re trying to understand how people decide what to wear and shop for clothes.  
I’m not selling anything — I just want to understand your experience.  
Be brutally honest — that helps the most.

---

## 2. Persona & Context (3–4 minutes)

- What do you do? (Student? Professional? Field?)
- How often do you think about what you’re wearing?
- How important is your appearance on a daily basis?
- Do you dress differently for school/work vs social events?
- How big would you say your wardrobe is?

---

## 3. Problem Exploration (Core Section – 7–10 minutes)

### Daily Dressing

- What’s the hardest part about getting dressed in the morning?
- Can you walk me through the last time that happened?
- How long does it usually take you to decide what to wear?
- Do you tend to rotate the same outfits?

### Wardrobe Awareness

- Do you ever forget what clothes you own?
- Have you ever bought something similar to what you already had?
- Do you feel like you have enough variety?
- Do some clothes go unworn for weeks?

### Fit & Confidence

- How important is fit compared to color or style?
- Have you had issues with sizing across brands?
- What’s the most frustrating clothing purchase you’ve made?
- Do you compare yourself to models or size charts when shopping?

### Shopping Behavior

- When was the last time you shopped for clothes?
- Was it online or in person?
- How long did you spend browsing?
- What websites or stores do you usually shop at?
- Have you ever returned something because it didn’t fit?
- Have you spent money trying to solve any clothing-related frustrations?

---

## 4. Current Solutions (Past Behavior = Truth)

- What do you currently do to make outfit decisions easier?
- Do you text friends for opinions?
- Do you rely on “default” outfits?
- Do you intentionally simplify your wardrobe?
- Have you tried any apps or tools for wardrobe tracking?

---

## 5. Solution Signal (3–4 minutes)

(Only after understanding their experience)

We’re exploring the idea of a digital wardrobe assistant that:

- Lets you see what you own
- Helps you visualize how clothes would look on you
- Suggests outfit combinations
- Tracks what you’ve worn recently

Initial reaction?

Follow-ups:
- Would this solve any of the problems you described?
- What would concern you about something like this?
- Would you want it to decide for you or just assist?
- Would you upload photos of your clothes?
- What would make it worth using regularly?

---

## 6. Commitment Questions

- If this existed today, would you try it?
- Would you pay for it? How much?
- What would stop you from using it?
- Do you know 1–2 other people who struggle with this?
- Would you be open to a follow-up conversation?

---

## 7. Magic Wand Question

If you could wave a magic wand and change one thing about how you:

- Get dressed
- Shop for clothes
- Track your wardrobe
- Find clothes that fit

What would it be?

---

# Interviewer Reminders

- Talk less than the interviewee (80/20 rule).
- Ask about the **past**, not hypotheticals.
- Listen for emotion (frustration, sighing, repetition).
- Capture exact quotes.
- Fill out snapshot within 1 hour.
